# Neural Shadow Art — Technical Overview

**Authors:** Caoliwen Wang, Bailin Deng, Juyong Zhang  
**Venue:** Pacific Graphics 2025 Conference Track  
**Paper:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.19161

---

## Problem Statement

Shadow art design is the inverse problem of finding a 3D sculpture such that its silhouette projections under multiple light sources match a set of target 2D images. Prior methods (voxel grids, meshes) suffer from resolution limitations, fixed topology, and inaccurate shadow reproduction. This paper reformulates the problem using a **neural implicit occupancy function**, enabling topology-free, resolution-independent geometry that is directly 3D-printable.

---

## Core Representation

The sculpture is represented as a neural occupancy function:

```
f_Θ,l,s : ℝ³ → [0, 1]
```

which maps a 3D point to its probability of being occupied. Architecture:

- **Positional encoding** γ(p) with L=6 frequency levels (following NeRF conventions)
- **8-layer MLP**, 256 channels per layer, ReLU activations, final sigmoid output
- **Jointly optimized parameters:** network weights Θ, light directions {l_i}, and screen orientations {s_i}

The light directions and screen orientations are optimized alongside the network weights, meaning non-perpendicular light–screen configurations are naturally supported — a significant advance over prior work.

---

## Differentiable Rendering Pipeline

### Ray Generation

For each input binary target image, rays are cast through the projection screen. Ray start/end points are derived from image pixel coordinates. The ray–screen distance is computed as `d / ⟨l_i, s_i⟩`, which correctly handles oblique (non-perpendicular) light directions.

### Ray Truncation

A critical design choice: all rays are **truncated to the intersection of all viewing frustums** generated by every projection constraint. This prevents geometry from growing outside the regions visible to any light source, which would otherwise cast spurious shadows.

### Predicted Occupancy

The occupancy along a ray is aggregated as:

```
O_j = 1 - ∏_k (1 - f(p_{j,k}))
```

This product formulation provides unbiased satisfaction of multiple shadow constraints simultaneously.

---

## Loss Functions

The total loss is a weighted sum of five terms:

```
L = β₀·L_ren + β₁·L_coh + β₂·L_smo + β₃·L_vol + β₄·L_bin
```

| Term | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **L_ren** (Rendering) | MSE between predicted ray occupancy and ground-truth shadow mask. Weighted by shadow region area ratio to prioritize smaller targets. |
| **L_coh** (Cohesion) | Penalizes occupancy changes between adjacent points along a ray, discouraging thin multi-layer geometry and encouraging contiguous solid regions. |
| **L_smo** (Smoothness) | Identifies surface points via gradient magnitude thresholding (`‖∇f‖ > θ_w`) and penalizes normal variation across the 26 nearest surface neighbors. |
| **L_vol** (Volume) | Minimizes total material via a differentiable soft-sigmoid approximation of voxel count. Avoids trivial cylindrical solutions that satisfy shadows with excess bulk. |
| **L_bin** (Binarization) | Forces occupancy toward binary values via `min(f(p)², (1−f(p))²)`, preventing semi-transparent artifacts. |

### Loss Scheduling

Weights β₁ and β₄ are ramped up early (scaled by `2^min(epoch, 3)`), allowing basic geometry to form first. Cohesion and smoothness losses (β₂, β₃) are activated only after epoch 3 so they refine rather than constrain early structure.

---

## Rigid Registration

When input shadow images have incompatible silhouettes (e.g., disconnected regions that cannot simultaneously arise from a single object), the method applies **rigid transformation** to projected geometries relative to target images. Light directions and screen orientations are jointly optimized to find a feasible configuration. This is absent from all prior methods.

---

## 3D Printing

After optimization converges, **Marching Cubes** is run on a 200³ grid at occupancy threshold τ = 0.5, producing a watertight mesh for fabrication. The volume loss directly supports the industrial requirement of minimizing material. The paper validates fabrication with physical 3D-printed prototypes that reproduce the intended shadow effects.

**Known limitation:** Disconnected silhouettes may produce floating geometry parts, requiring either translucent embedding material or manual connecting rods.

## Comparison to Prior Work

| Capability | Mitra & Pauly 2009 | Sadekar et al. 2022 | **Neural Shadow Art** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Representation | Voxels | Voxels / Mesh | Neural implicit |
| Arbitrary topology | No | No | **Yes** |
| Resolution-free | No | No | **Yes** |
| Flexible light/screen angles | No | No | **Yes** |
| Rigid registration | No | No | **Yes** |
| Volume minimization | No | No | **Yes** |

**Mitra & Pauly (2009):** Voxel carving with ARAP deformation — produces notable discrepancies between projections and targets.

**Sadekar et al. (2022):** Differentiable rendering on voxels/meshes — resolution-constrained, topology-locked, no light optimization.

## Results

### Quantitative (Table 2 — IoU / Dice across Animal, Currency, Music test cases)

| Method | IoU | Dice |
|---|---|---|
| Voxel-based (prior) | 0.876 | — |
| **Neural Shadow Art** | **0.952–0.967** | **0.975–0.983** |

### Volume Efficiency (Table 3)

Generates **22–41% less volume** and **18–37% less surface area** than voxel-based baselines.

### Ablation Studies

- Removing **volume loss** → significantly redundant geometry
- Removing **cohesion/smoothness** → multi-layered or inconsistent surfaces
- Removing **ray truncation** → large spurious structures degrade shadow quality
- Removing **registration or light optimization** → measurable IoU/Dice drop

### Training Cost

~1.5 hours per geometry on an NVIDIA RTX 3090 (24 GB VRAM) for 30 epochs.

## Limitations & Future Work

- Disconnected input silhouettes may produce non-manifold floating parts
- Training is GPU-intensive (~1.5h per sculpture)
- No official code release at time of publication
- Future directions implied: handling transparent/translucent materials, multi-color shadows, real-time preview
